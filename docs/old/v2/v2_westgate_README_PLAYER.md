# Welcome to Westgate Heights

**A new neighborhood. Four homes. Your name on the deed.**

---

You've slept in mud. You've slept in inns. You've slept in the corners
of caves with one eye open and a hand on your sword.

What if, just for a few hours, you slept in a bed that was *yours?*

---

## The Land Beyond the Gate

Word came down the Ados road on the back of a wool-merchant's cart:
**Mira Penhollow** has cleared the meadow west of the gate. She's built
a market square. She's strung up lanterns and run cobblestone paths
between the stalls. And she's selling **four versions of a personal
home** to anyone with the gold to take one.

When you push west out of Ados's old timber gate now, the world doesn't
end at the road. It opens up. There's smoke from a stove. There's
hammering from an anvil. Children chase chickens between the seamstress
and the grocer. A real-estate agent in a long blue coat is waiting to
shake your hand.

This is **Westgate Heights**.

---

## Your Adventure Awaits

Westgate Market hums from morning to dusk. Six stalls form a horseshoe
around the square — and every merchant has something you'll want.

- The **Weaponsmith** sells iron, steel, bronze, hafts and grips. Take
  them home and you can pound out blades a quartermaster would envy.
- The **Armorsmith** stocks ingots of every metal — silver, gold,
  copper — and polished gemstones for the rings you'll forge.
- The **Seamstress** stretches bolts of linen, wool, and silk, and
  bundles thread, needles, and dye in tidy little bags.
- The **Grocer** has flour, butter, milk, eggs, raw meat and fresh fish
  laid out under wax cloth.
- The **Farmers Market** runs riot with carrots, cabbages, tomatoes,
  apples, strawberries — anything the local farms picked this week.
- The **Gift & Sundries** stall sells what you forgot to pack: a
  **portable stove** you can fold under your arm, fuel charges, dried
  rations, candles, all the small things that turn a long road into a
  bearable one.

Beside the farmers market stands **Old Tomek**, who sells **24 kinds of
seed** — every vegetable, fruit, and herb that grows in the western
fields. Plant them. Wait two days. Eat what you grow.

## Four Homes. Four Lives.

> *"Pick the one that fits the size of your dreams. The deed will be in
>  your hand by sundown."* — Mira Penhollow

### The Cottage — 5,000 gold
One snug room with a treadle **sewing machine** by the window. Stitch
your own hood. Sew your own boots. Walk out wearing something nobody
else in Stendhal owns.

### The House — 12,000 gold
Two rooms. The sewing nook from the Cottage, plus a real kitchen with
a **cast-iron stove**. Bake bread. Cook stew. Roast a chicken with
herbs from the market. **Twelve recipes** to learn.

### The Manor — 25,000 gold
Three rooms. Now you have a **forge** in the craft hall — your own
anvil, your own quench barrel — and a **garden plot** out back. **24
seed varieties** in your bag. **12 weapons, 6 shields, and 12 rings**
within reach of your hammer. Plant your dinner. Forge your blade.
Sleep under a roof you paid for in gold and sweat.

### The Estate — 50,000 gold
Four rooms. Everything above, plus eight garden tiles instead of four,
and one more thing money can buy: **Reginald**.

> *"Good day, master. Reginald, at your service."*

Reginald is your butler. He has a standing arrangement with every stall
in the market. Every ingredient, every ingot, every seed — Reginald
fetches at **a 20% discount**. He never tires. He never gossips. The
estate is yours, the discount is yours, and the day is yours.

## Things You Can Make

**15 garments** to sew — from a humble linen hood to an embroidered
silk sash that catches the light when you turn.

**12 hot meals** to cook — bread loaves, vegetable stew, roast
chicken, meat pies, grilled fish, berry tarts, hearty soup, sausages,
cheese bake, honey cake, traveler's rations, garden omelettes. Each
restores your health. Some restore stamina too.

**24 plants** to grow — carrots, potatoes, cabbages, tomatoes, corn,
wheat, barley, onions, garlic, peppers, pumpkins, cucumbers, lettuce,
spinach, beans, peas, radishes, beets, strawberries, watermelons,
apples, pears, grapes, fresh herbs.

**12 weapons** to forge — daggers, shortswords, longswords, battleaxes,
maces, warhammers, spears, halberds, scimitars, rapiers, clubs, and
finally a great two-handed steel greatsword that takes five ingots and
three lumps of coal to make and clears a room when you swing it.

**6 shields** to forge — buckler, wood, round, kite, tower, heater.

**12 rings** to forge — plain bands of every metal, plus six gemstone
rings that make you stronger, faster, luckier, wiser, harder to hit,
or simply heavier in the swing.

---

## Why You'll Stay

Most adventures end with a treasure chest. This one ends with a roof,
a garden, and a kitchen that smells like honey cake.

You'll come back to Westgate Heights between dungeons. You'll plant
strawberries before you leave for the swamp and you'll pick them when
you stagger home. You'll sew a new cloak the night before a long march.
You'll forge a steel rapier as the sun comes up. You'll send Reginald
out for cheese while you take a hot bath.

It's not a side-quest. It's a place you live.

**Pack your gold. Walk west. The neighborhood is waiting.**

## And One More Thing — The Castle on the Hill

Just north of the market road, behind a low stone wall and a row of
elder trees, **Westgate Castle** has thrown open its doors.

It belongs to **Queen Sunny**, who has heard of your exploits and
wants to meet you herself. Push north through the entry hall, past
Doorman Heric, and you'll find a small kingdom inside: a Royal Grove
and walled garden, a workroom busy with quills and ink, a Trophy Hall
hung with relics from old wars, a vast Library, two wings of guest
quarters, and the throne room itself, where the queen waits in a hall
lit by stained glass.

There are **fifteen rooms** to explore on the main floor alone —
laid out in a 5-by-3 grid you can wander at your own pace. Take the
stairs down from the Great Hall and you'll find the **lower castle**
— armory, servant quarters, storage room, and the off-duty guards'
quarters around a wide central hall. Go further down still and you
reach the **dungeons**: warden's office, a second armory, a guard
office, and four prison cells. (One of the prisoners has a story to
tell. Listen.)

From the entry hall, take the stairs **up** instead and you'll come
out on the **castle roof**. The wind cuts a little. Four corner
towers ring the roof, and each one has a **lookout level** above with
a watchman who can see weather coming from miles off.

And in the row of guest rooms on the main floor, on the right side of
the central entry, there is one door with your name on it. **Jasia's
bedroom.** Your bed. Your wardrobe. Your window. Sleep here and you
will rise at full health, ready to walk back out into the kingdom.

There are three errands the castle will ask of you when you're ready —
a missing silver set, a tower drill, and a prisoner with a sealed
letter. Help where you can. The queen does not forget those who help.

**Push north past the homes. Walk in. The castle is yours to know.**
